01From the showroom floor

Premium or Limited: the short answer.

Pick Premium if you want the strongest dollar-for-feature value and you are fine with StarTex® water-repellent upholstery and a simpler interior. Pick Limited if you want leather-trimmed seating, more standard convenience and safety content, and a quieter, more finished cabin. Neither changes the core all-wheel-drive Outback character.

Both trims share Subaru Symmetrical All-Wheel Drive as standard. Both include the EyeSight Driver Assist suite on current Outback models. The gap is content and finish, not capability.

03The value pick

Who should choose Premium.

Premium is the trim most cross-shoppers land on when budget leads the decision. You keep all-wheel drive, the EyeSight suite, and the usable cargo area that makes the Outback an Outback. You give up leather and some of the convenience extras that come standard higher up.

Premium suits a commuter, a weekend trail-and-camp driver, or a family that wants Subaru capability without paying for the top comfort tier. It is the practical pick.

04The comfort pick

Who should choose Limited.

Limited is for the shopper who spends real time in the seat. Leather-trimmed upholstery, more standard convenience features, and a more isolated cabin make a difference on a long National City to Los Angeles run. If you keep your vehicle eight or ten years, the comfort content earns its place.

Limited also tends to hold a cleaner used-market story because of the higher standard content. For resale value framing on either trim, compare data at kbb.com.

08Trim-shopper questions

Frequently asked questions about Outback Premium vs Limited.

What is the main difference between Subaru Outback Premium and Limited?

The main difference is comfort and standard content. Premium uses StarTex® water-repellent upholstery and a core feature set, while Limited adds leather-trimmed upholstery and more standard convenience and safety features.

Both keep standard all-wheel drive on the same Outback platform.

Is the Outback Limited worth the extra over Premium?

It depends on how you use the vehicle. If you spend long hours in the seat or keep your car many years, the leather-trimmed seats and added comfort content in the Limited often justify the step up.

If budget leads, Premium delivers the strongest value.

Do both Outback Premium and Limited come with all-wheel drive?

Yes. Subaru Symmetrical All-Wheel Drive is standard across the Outback lineup, including both Premium and Limited.

For the current model-year specifics, confirm on subaru.com before you buy.
